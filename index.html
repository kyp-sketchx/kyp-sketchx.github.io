About Me:

I defended my PhD at Queen Mary University of London in 2020, advised by Prof. Yi-Zhe Song and Prof. Tao Xiang. Before that, I received the first-class B.S. degree from the Sino-UK Joint Programme held between Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications and Queen Mary University of London 2016. I delivered the valedictory speech to the class of 2016.

I do research on computer vision and machine learning, with a specialisation on computational modelling of human sketch data. See this survey article accepted by TPAMI'21 on how sketch research has transformed the world of fine-grained visual analysis.

I am a strong advocate for human-centred AI. I develop AI solutions to help understand the deep needs, aspirations and drives that underlies humans in their unique sense of beings. I mix qualitative and quantitative research methods to better understand how humans perceive technology, and what they need when interacting and collaborating with it. For a high-level overview of my research interests, see here.

I start a day with coffee. I like neo-classic (piano) music, which takes the best bits of classical music, often in a spirit of minimalism, and mixes them up into a delicious sound salad. I enjoy watching Nature/Wildlife documentary (BBC Earth for example). The raw but profound power of nature always transforms my perception of the mundane, and helps me to find happiness in sorrow.
